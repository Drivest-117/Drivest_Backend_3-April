This Agreement governs the relationship between Drivest Limited and instructors using the platform.

1. Platform role
Drivest is a platform that may provide marketplace visibility, scheduling tools, lesson requests, policy records, payment support, and related administrative controls.
Driving instruction is provided by the instructor, not by Drivest.

2. Independent business status
You use Drivest as an independent instructor business.
No employment, partnership, franchise, or agency relationship is created between you and Drivest.

3. Instructor responsibilities
You are responsible for lawful instruction, licences, approvals, insurance, vehicle condition, profile accuracy, pricing accuracy, legal compliance, lesson delivery, tax obligations, and your own professional conduct.
You must keep your information accurate and up to date.

4. Profile review and marketplace approval
Drivest may review, approve, reject, suspend, restrict, or remove instructor marketplace visibility where reasonably necessary for safety, fraud prevention, legal compliance, quality control, or platform integrity.
Profile visibility may remain pending until review is complete.

5. Payments, commission, and cancellations
Drivest may collect payments on behalf of instructors for marketplace bookings.
Commission is recorded at 8 percent on completed marketplace lessons only.
No Drivest commission is retained on cancellations.
Cancellation and reschedule outcomes follow the platform policy and the booking context shown at the time of booking.

6. Payouts and holds
Payouts are subject to lesson completion, dispute status, payment provider settlement, fraud review, chargeback management, and policy or compliance review.
Timing is not guaranteed.
Drivest may delay, hold, adjust, or reverse payouts where reasonably necessary for dispute handling, fraud prevention, legal compliance, provider instructions, or platform integrity.

7. Platform enforcement
Drivest may suspend or terminate accounts, limit marketplace activity, or apply other operational restrictions where reasonably necessary for fraud, misuse, safety concerns, abusive conduct, repeated complaints, payment issues, chargebacks, or legal risk.

8. Governing law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales.
